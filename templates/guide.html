Week 2-3

Justification

Justification is God's process of cleaning us so that we are fully righteous; not by our own works but by Jesus' sacrifice. It's relevance in the doctrine of salvation is so that we may one day restore our connection with God. This is done through the imputed nature of Christ's righteousness

  • Ask about the definition of imputed
  • Consider asking why we need justification in the first place
  • Ask about why we can't be righteous out of our own works and efforts

In the process of justification there are negative and positive aspects of justification

  • Ask about what are some of the negative aspects of justification
  • Ask about what are some of the positive aspects of justification

There are two main ways we are justified through Christ's righteousness

  • Ask it about the difference between imputed and imparted in the context of Justification

The end result of justification is Peace with God, Salvation from God's wrath and glorification.

Adoption

Adoption is a beautiful concept in theology that speaks to our relationship with God. It's not just about being chosen; it's about being welcomed into God's family as His children. With this new identity come both privileges and responsibilities.

  • Ask the AI about the the difference between adoption and conversion
  • Inquire about the significance being adopted by God has

As we are adopted we are granted certain privileges such as a community and a future inheritance from our Father

  • Consider what type of inheritance the Father will leave us with
  • What type of community are we adopted into?
  • What role does the holy spirit play in perfecting our relationships?

Alongside these benefits we also bear new responsibilities such as bearing the family name/reputation of the God's name.

  • Ask what it means to bear God's family name and reputation
  • Consider how being a child of God influences our choices and behaviors.

Week 3-4

Sanctification

Sanctification is about the process of becoming more Christ-like in our lives. This is achieved through God's grace which leads us to depart from the life of sin.

  • Ask the AI what is the life of sin
  • Ask the AI What it means to be Christ-like

There are two main ways of viewing sanctification. Progressive and Positional

Progressive Sanctification is an ongoing process of growing in righteousness, guided by the Holy Spirit and obeying God's will.

  • Ask the AI What are some Bible Verses about progressive sanctification
  • Ask the AI how obeying God's will makes us more Christ-like

Positional Sanctification is the status we receive at the moment of regeneration, where we are declared holy and set apart by God for his purposes

  • Ask the AI about Bible Verses which support Positional Sanctification
  • Ask it how Progressive and Positional sanctification is different and where it is the same

Sanctification's process can be understood between Imputation vs. Impartation

  • Ask the AI how Sanctification's Imputation and Impartation differs from Justification's Imputation and Impartation

The different views is that Imputation refers to God's righteousness being attributed to us in justification. While Impartation is the process of Christ's righteousness being bestowed upon us in sanctification.

  • Ask the AI how sanctification enables us to love more genuinely and selflessly

Perseverance

Perseverance is about both God's role in preserving believers and the believers' responsibility to continue in their faith.

  • Ask the AI to explain the concept of perseverance in Christian theology.
  • Inquire about the two aspects of perseverance: God preserving believers and believers' ongoing perseverance in Christ.

Similar to a relationship, both parties must preserve the friendship if they want it maintain the bond. The same goes for a relationship with God. God preserves by keeping believers secure in their faith.

  • Consider asking about the significance of this assurance for a believer's confidence in their salvation.

Believers are called to persist in their faith through God's grace, which sustains and increases their faith to the end.

  • Consider the role of obedience to God's commandments in persevering in His love.
  • Consider asking how misconceptions such as "once saved, always saved" can be debunked

Glorification

Glorification is the culmination of the Christian journey, where believers are fully transformed into the likeness of Christ and enjoy eternal communion with God.

  • Ask the AI to define glorification in the context of Christian soteriology.
  • Inquire about the biblical basis for glorification and its significance in the believer's life.
  • Explore the Biblical Texts and Explanations talking about Glorification

There are three key aspects of Glorification

  • Glorification is the final goal of sanctification
  • Glorification includes the renewal of creation
  • Glorification is associated with Christ’s coming in glory
